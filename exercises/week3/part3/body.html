For all three points I create the intrinsics of the camera, mat_in, using the perspective function with fovy=45 degrees and equal aspect ratio.

One-point

Here I add the camera extrinsics to the camera matrix, mat_ex. I do this by placing the camera at the center of the cube and moving it away by 4.5 units along the z-axis. Lastly I move the object on the pixel plane by translating it to the left, mat_trans.
output = mat_trans * mat_in * mat_ex * cube

Two-point

Here I do the same as in the first case, except I place the camera to the side instead of the front and move the cube a little to the rigth in the pixel plane.

Three-point

Here I do the exact same again, except I palce the camera at the corner of the cube.

Implementation 

        
// render
gl.clear(gl.COLOR_BUFFER_BIT)
var modelViewMatrixLoc = gl.getUniformLocation(program, "modelViewMatrix")

// see from the front
modelViewMatrix = mult(
    cameraMatrix,
    lookAt(
        vec3(0.5, 0.5, 5),
        vec3(0.5, 0.5, 0.0),
        vec3(0.0, 1.0, 0.0)))
modelViewMatrix = mult(translate(-0.6, 0, 0), modelViewMatrix)
gl.uniformMatrix4fv(modelViewMatrixLoc, false, flatten(modelViewMatrix));
gl.drawElements(gl.LINES, wire_indices.length, gl.UNSIGNED_INT, 0)

// from the side
modelViewMatrix = mult(
    cameraMatrix,
    lookAt(
        vec3(0.5, 5, 5),
        vec3(0.5, 0, 0.0),
        vec3(0.0, 1.0, 0.0)))
modelViewMatrix = mult(translate(0.1, 0, 0), modelViewMatrix)
gl.uniformMatrix4fv(modelViewMatrixLoc, false, flatten(modelViewMatrix));
gl.drawElements(gl.LINES, wire_indices.length, gl.UNSIGNED_INT, 0)

// see from the corner
modelViewMatrix = mult(
    cameraMatrix,
    lookAt(
        vec3(5, 5, 5),
        vec3(0.0, 0.0, 0.0),
        vec3(0.0, 1.0, 0.0)))
modelViewMatrix = mult(translate(0.7, 0, 0), modelViewMatrix)
gl.uniformMatrix4fv(modelViewMatrixLoc, false, flatten(modelViewMatrix))
gl.drawElements(gl.LINES, wire_indices.length, gl.UNSIGNED_INT, 0)
